THE BEST FRENCH MACARON

Recipe by: Miranda Barriero

Macarons (ma-kr-aan) originated in Italy but was brought over to France in the 16th century when Catherine di Medici married Henri II (future King of France). What does it mean? Macaron comes from the Italian word, maccherone, which translates to fine dough. Macarons are small sandwiches made of meringue, almond flour and buttercream filling. These elegant sandwiches can come in many different colors and flavors. Therefore, macarons make for a great pastry to have fun with!

  • Quantity: 30 macarons
  • Total Time: 1 hour 50 mins
  • Prep time: 30 mins
  • Cook Time: 20 mins

MACARONS

  • 1 3/4 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 cup almond flour (finely ground)
  • 1 tsp salt (divided)
  • 3 egg whites (room temperature)
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 2 drops gel food coloring (your choice of color!)

VANILLA BUTTERCREAM

  • 1 cup unsalted butter (room temperature)
  • 3 cups powdered sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 tbsps heavy cream

STEPS TO BAKE

MACARONS

  1. Combine powdered sugar, almond flour and 1/2 tsp salt in food processor on low speed until extra fine. Sift the mixture through a fine-mesh sleeve into a bowl.
  2. In a seperate bowl, beat egg whites and the other 1/2 tsp of salt until soft peaks form. Add the granulated sugar gradually.
  3. Add the vanilla and beat.
  4. Add the food coloring and beat.
  5. Add 1/3 of the sifted almond flour mixture at a time to the beaten egg whites and gently fold with a spatula.
  6. Put macaron batter into a rounded-tip piping bag.
  7. Place 4 dots of the batter in each corner of a rimmed baking sheet and put a piece of parchment paper over it.
  8. Pipe the macarons onto the paper. Should be 1 1/2 inch (3 cm) circles, spacing at least 1-inch (2 cm) apart.
  9. Tap the baking sheet on a flat surface a few times to release air bubbles.
  10. Let macarons sit at room temperature for 30 minutes to 1 hour until they are dry to the touch.
  11. Preheat the oven to 300 degrees (farenheit).
  12. Bake macarons for 17 minutes, until well-risen and the macarons don't stick to the parchment paper.
  13. Transfer macarons to a wire rack to cool.

BUTTERCREAM

  1. Add the butter and beat with a mixer for 1 minute until light and fluffy. Sift in the powdered sugar and beat. Add the vanilla and beat. Add the cream (1 tbsp at a time) and beat.
  2. Transfer buttercream to a rounded-tip piping bag.

PUT IT TOGETHER!

  1. Pipe the buttercream to one macaron shell. Top it with another macaron shell to create a sandwich!
  2. Repeat, repeat, repeat!
  3. Place in an airtight container for 24 hours to "bloom".
